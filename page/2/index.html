Review - Is advance knowledge of flow sizes a plausible assumption?

Posted on 2019-03-26 | Edited on 2019-10-12 | In Paper Review

Summary the paper

This work presents FLUX, a framework applying both simple heuristics and machine
learning methods to estimate the flows’ sizes in advance. Evaluations show that
even simple heuristics can achieve high estimation precision and the partial knowledge
aquired by FLUX can benefit a number of flow scheduling for data center networks.

What motivates the work

To better achieve SLAs in data center networks, a number of flow scheduling mechanisms
have been proposed. Among these works, some (e.g. pFabric, PDQ, D3, FastPass, pHost,
Homa, Varys) assume the full knowledge of flow/coflow sizes, while others (e.g. PIAS,
Aalo) schedule flows without such knowledge, claiming to sacrifice certain performance
to benefit and scale deployment.
